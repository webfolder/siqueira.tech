Experiences

Intern at Samsung

I entered at Samsung in 2014 as an intern, after a long selection process. In my first day in the company I was challenged to create an Android application from scratch, even I had never coded anything in this environment. My strategy was divided my time in two parts: learn Android and develop the project. Firstly, I have created a paper prototype with the main idea of the application, I did it, because I had a short time and I could not waste time programming wrong requirements. Secondly, I modeled the entity-relationship of my database (I have used the Chen notation), at the same time, I created the class diagram. During my activities I have kept my focus to create a strong architecture, consequently I have applied some design patterns, following: MVC, Strategy, Observer, Method Factory, Singleton, and Active Record. In the half of my internship, my Android skills have improved, but not enough for accomplishing of the project. In order to finish my tasks and keep the quality, I decided to do overtime. At that moment, I have coded many hours per day, but I always kept my attention on my code appearance, documentation (I have used Doxygen), and the use of SDK tools instead of using IDE (I have used adb, logcat, ant, and vim as my base tools). At the end of my internship, I had to defend my project in front of many engineers that I have never seen in my life. Fortunately, everybody likes my project and I successfully concluded my internship.

Intern, AEG - Brazil

AEG is a small company in Brazil, which provides business consultancy to other companies. When I worked for AEG, they needed a web system for handling their client's complaints and suggestions. In this sense, I was designated to create a software for this situation. Firstly, I understood the requirements and then model the system with UML diagram. I have created the system database with MySQL 4.0. Additionally I had coded and tested the application written in PHP 4.3, and running under Apache 2.3.
